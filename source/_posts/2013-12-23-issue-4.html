Square - How Did Square Grow So Quickly? - GrowthHackers

Tagged: growthhacking, square, techweekly, casestudy
Square is a US based startup that offers a sexy and easy way to accept credit card payments via your smart phone. The article describes how Square initially got a lot of traction because of founder Jack Dorsey (one of the founders of Twitter) but then quickly built itself a sustainable engine of growth. Key elements for growth: compelling value proposition, an elegant piece of hardware and raving fans.

The Angel VC: The 8th DO for SaaS startups - Stay on top of your KPIs

Tagged: SaaS, metrics, techweekly
Everybody talks about analytics and metrics, but not all metrics matter, at all time. This article presents an interesting frame that SaaS companies can use to look at which metrics are important for them during, at different stages in the life of the company: pre-product/market fit, your most important metrics to track are user feedback and signups. Once you're reaching P/M-fit, engagement starts to play a much more prominent role and so on. Plenty of food for thought, especially for the SaaS entrepreneurs among us.

Full article: The Angel VC: The 8th DO for SaaS startups - Stay on top of your KPIs"

How One SaaS Startup Reduced Churn 71% Using Red Flag Metrics

Tagged: churn, analytics, saas, techweekly
Indepth article on how to detect and fight churn for your SaaS business. I particularly like how they use analytics and cohort analysis to detect some red flags for churn, and how they then use transactional and personalized emails to interact with users that are most likely churners. Inspiring stuff. If you know more stories like this, please share them with me.

How was SnapChat able to grow so quickly? | DFG LabsDFG Labs

Tagged: techweekly, snapshat, growth
This article points out 5 reasons why Snapchat, a photo sharing application, is growing so fast. One: It's a communication app, so inherently there is a network effect. Two: Photo sharing is an activity done by millions. Snapchat reduces friction in this sharing process. Stickiness, fun and mobile are reasons number 3,4 and 5.
