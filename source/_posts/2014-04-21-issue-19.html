Growth vs Marketing vs Product — Brian Balfour's coElevate

Tagged: growth, marketing, growthhacking, techweekly
I know, it's Easter Monday, a holiday in much of Europe. Therefor, only 2 articles this week, but I chose some larger onces, that put things in perspective. This arcile by @bbalfour is about growth, and puts things like growth, growth hacking, marketing and product into perspective. His biggest point: 5 years ago, analytics and metrics were in their infancy, so most growth happened by accident. Now, with tools like mix panel, kissmetrics etc. one has analytics, and thus insights, throughout the whole funnel, from acquisitions to retention and back. This gave rise to marketing teams that take a holistic approach to growth, including product. Interesting read.

Growth Hacking Free Trials: Time to Wow! is the key to success

Tagged: growthhacking, startups, saas, techweekly
You'll need some time for this article, it's lengthy, but it's worth every minute. This article introduced the "time to wow" concept: imagine you are offering a SaaS, and you offer a free trail period for your users, try before you buy. You better be sure that, during this free trail period, these trail users will experience a real "wow" moment, so they really appreciate your application, and thus convert into buying customers. Your job is to engineer this on boarding process (and the whole trail experience) such that as much obstacles are removed between the first user experience and the "wow" moment. Sounds logical, isn't it? Well, what are you waiting for, start rethinking your on boarding and trail process and start optimising.
