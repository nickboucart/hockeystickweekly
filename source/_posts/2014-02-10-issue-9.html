A Landing Page Is NOT A Minimum Viable Product | Ramli John

Tagged: landingpage, leanstartup, mvp, techweekly
I found this a great article. I too think every now and then that a landing page often is an MVP 0.1. In this article, @ramlyjohn argues that a landing page more often than not is not an MVP, because it doesn't really solve a customer problem and does not score high on the "validated learning" department. The author does give a couple of alternatives, from a "blog MVP", an email MVP to even a "hustle" MVP.

Two big announcements.

Tagged: basecamp, 37signals, saas, techweekly
This week, the guys of 37 signals made a couple of strong announcements. For those who don't know 37 signals, these are the guys and galls behind BaseCamp and Ruby on Rails. Their announcements in short: they will rebrand themselves to BaseCamp, and will only focus on Basecamp, the product, phasing out the rest of their product portfolio. So, rather than growing the company much larger in terms of people in order to support their whole product portfolio, they chose to focus, keep it relatively small and have the kind of company they always wanted to have. Respect.
